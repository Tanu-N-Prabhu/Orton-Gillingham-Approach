# Grade 5 Writing 
## Hamburger Method Practice  
**Date:** December 2, 2025  
**Duration:** 1 Hour  
**Focus:** Slow, neat, structured writing with clear intro-details-conclusion.

---

## Learning Goal  
Write a well-organized paragraph using the **Hamburger Method**:
- **Top Bun:** Introduction sentence  
- **Meat (3-5 sentences):** Details, examples, descriptions  
- **Bottom Bun:** Concluding sentence  

---

## Part 1: Warm-Up (10 minutes)  
Rewrite the messy sentence neatly and slowly:  

**Sentence:**  
> the cat sat by the window and watched the birds fly past  

**Tasks:**  
1. Capitalize correctly  
2. Add punctuation  
3. Add 1–2 adjectives  
4. Write it neatly once

---

## Part 2: Main Writing Task (25 minutes) – Hamburger Paragraph  
Choose ONE topic and write a **Hamburger Paragraph**.

### Writing Topics (Choose One)
1. **My favorite place to relax**  
2. **Why recess is important**  
3. **A pet I wish I had**  
4. **My dream weekend**  
5. **The best meal I ever ate**

### Writing Expectations
- **1 Intro sentence**  
- **3–5 detail sentences (goal = 5)**  
- **1 concluding sentence**  
- Write *slowly* and keep letters evenly spaced

---

## Part 3: Slow, Neat Writing Drill (10 minutes)  
Copy the following sentence **twice**, focusing on neatness and even spacing:

> Writing slowly helps my sentences look clean, clear, and easy to read.

---

## Part 4: Mini Challenge – Expand the “Meat” (10 minutes)  
Turn the short detail sentence into a longer, more descriptive one.

**Short sentence:**  
> The park is fun.

**Expand it into 3 better versions:**  
- Add adjectives  
- Add sensory details (what you see/hear/feel)  
- Add an emotion

Examples Noah can try:  
- “The big, green park is fun because I can run on the cool grass.”  
- “The park is fun when I play soccer with my friends after school.”  
- “The park near my house is fun because it always feels bright and peaceful.”

---

## Homework (Optional)
